Luke Gloege

NASA Postdoctoral Fellow

I am a climate scientist based at the NASA Goddard Institute for Space Studies in New York City. My reserch focuses on understanding changes in the global carbon cycle using models, direct observations, and satellite retrievals. Current research is at the nexus of machine learning and Earth science. I am particularly interested in how biology and physics mediate the spatial patterns of nutrients and pCO2 to understand how the climate and ecosystems change over time. This work can lead to efficient managment efforts in large lakes/oceans and reduce uncertainty in the global carbon budget.
